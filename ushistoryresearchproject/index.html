Why the American Revolution is inevitable
due to the various Enlightenment ideals

Continue

History is often substantially impacted by the ideals of the philosophical movements in
that era. What made the 17th century scientists so prolific was not that they are more
intelligent compared to the medieval scientists, but that they were born in an era that
promotes science and logic. Similarly, revolutions in the Age of Enlightenment were not
merely caused by the tyrannical governments.

While what directly contributed to the American Revolution was Britain’s disrespect of the
colonists’ rights and the inappropriately distributed power of the Parliament, the root of
the revolution was the rebellious ideologies originated in various Enlightenment thinkers
and spread in the colonies in 1760s and 1770s.

Ideologies shown by the Continental congress

Enraged by the Intolerable Acts, the representatives from the 13 colonies met to resist the British laws. Analyzing the documents from the continental congress, we can see the connections between the ideals of the colonists and the Enlightenment philosophers.
